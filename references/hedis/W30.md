# W30 — Well-Child Visits in the First 30 Months of Life

**Reporting path:** Admin
**Population focus:** Children in the first 30 months of life
**Sub-indicators (each evaluated independently):**
1. **W30-0-15** — Well-Child Visits in the First 15 Months of Life: **6 or more** well-child visits
2. **W30-15-30** — Well-Child Visits for Age 15 Months - 30 Months: **2 or more** well-child visits

## Denominator

### W30-0-15
- Children who turned 15 months old during MY
- Continuously enrolled from 31 days of age through 15 months (with allowed enrollment gap per spec)

### W30-15-30
- Children who turned 30 months old during MY
- Continuously enrolled from 15 months through 30 months (with allowed enrollment gap per spec)

## Numerator

### W30-0-15
- **6 or more well-child visits** with a PCP between 31 days and 15 months of age
- Each visit must be a well-child visit (not a sick visit)

### W30-15-30
- **2 or more well-child visits** with a PCP between 15 months and 30 months of age

## Exclusions

- Hospice
- Death during MY
- Per current spec - verify

## What counts as a well-child visit

- AAP / Bright Futures preventive visit (typically billed with well-child preventive codes)
- Documentation must show preventive care content: developmental surveillance, anticipatory guidance, immunizations review, growth tracking, physical exam

**Recommended AAP schedule for context:**
- Newborn, 3-5 days, by 1 month, 2 months, 4 months, 6 months, 9 months, 12 months, 15 months → satisfies 6+ for W30-0-15
- 18 months, 24 months, 30 months → satisfies 2+ for W30-15-30

## NLP signal phrases

**Section hints:** Encounter type, Chief Complaint, Assessment, Plan, Anticipatory Guidance section, growth chart, immunization tab

**Positive signals - well-child visit identification**
- "well-child visit" / "WCV" / "well-baby check"
- "[N]-month well-child check"
- "preventive visit"
- "health supervision visit"
- "Bright Futures visit"
- Age-appropriate language: "2-month visit", "9-month check", "18-month well"
- "anticipatory guidance provided regarding [age-appropriate topics]"

**Positive signals - well-child content components**
- "developmental surveillance" / "developmental milestones reviewed"
- "ASQ" / "Ages and Stages Questionnaire" / "M-CHAT-R" (autism screen at 18-24m)
- "growth chart updated" / "length, weight, head circumference plotted"
- "immunizations reviewed and administered per AAP"
- "anticipatory guidance: safe sleep / feeding / car seat / screen time / dental"
- "review of systems negative"
- "complete physical exam"

**Negative / sick-visit signals (DO NOT count)**
- "acute visit" / "sick visit" / "URI follow-up"
- "fever check"
- "ear infection recheck"
- "rash evaluation"
- Single-issue focused visits without preventive content

**False positives to filter**
- "well visit" billed but documentation is purely problem-focused
- Visit with non-PCP provider (e.g., specialist) without PCP attribution
- "appointment scheduled" without visit completed
- Bundled visits (sick + well same day) where well component is undocumented

## Common documentation gaps

- Sick visit + well visit combined; well-visit components not separately documented or coded
- Visit was preventive in nature but billed with problem code only
- Outside-PCP visits (e.g., urgent care for fever then preventive issues addressed) not captured as well-child
- Patient moves between plans; prior plan's visits not in current admin data
- Telehealth well-child visits acceptance varies by spec and component requirements (e.g., physical exam component)

## Notes

- **Both sub-indicators are reported as separate rates** - a child may close W30-0-15 but fail W30-15-30 (or vice versa)
- The age windows are **strict** - visits before 31 days or after the upper bound do NOT count
- AAP Bright Futures schedule aligns with the 6-visit and 2-visit minimums when followed properly
- ECDS direction: structured well-child encounters via FHIR `Encounter` with preventive type code
