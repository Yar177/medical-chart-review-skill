# Lab & Imaging Quick Reference

> Reference ranges vary by lab. Always check the lab's own reference range and flag values, not memorized ones. Critical/panic values trigger callback policies.

## Common labs — typical adult ranges

| Test | Typical range | Critical-low | Critical-high |
|---|---|---|---|
| Na | 135–145 mEq/L | <120 | >160 |
| K | 3.5–5.0 | <2.5 | >6.5 |
| Cl | 98–107 | — | — |
| HCO3 | 22–29 | <10 | >40 |
| BUN | 7–20 mg/dL | — | >100 |
| Creatinine | 0.6–1.3 (sex-dependent) | — | >5 (acute) |
| Glucose | 70–99 fasting | <40 | >500 |
| Ca (total) | 8.5–10.5 | <6 | >13 |
| Mg | 1.7–2.4 | <1 | >5 |
| Phos | 2.5–4.5 | <1 | >9 |
| WBC | 4.0–11.0 ×10³ | <2 | >30 |
| Hgb | 12–17 (sex) | <7 | >20 |
| Plt | 150–400 ×10³ | <20 | >1000 |
| INR (on warfarin) | goal 2–3 (or 2.5–3.5) | — | >5 |
| Troponin | assay-dependent | — | any rise/fall + sx = ACS |
| Lactate | <2 mmol/L | — | >4 (sepsis) |
| TSH | 0.4–4.5 mIU/L | — | — |
| A1c | <5.7% normal; ≥6.5% DM | — | — |
| LDL | goal varies by ASCVD risk | — | — |
| eGFR | >60 normal | — | <15 = stage 5 |

## Imaging common abbreviations

- **CXR, KUB, AXR**: chest, kidney-ureter-bladder, abdominal X-ray
- **CT A/P, CTA, HRCT**: abdomen/pelvis, angiography, high-resolution
- **MRI, MRA, MRV, MRCP**: angio, veno, cholangiopancreatography
- **US, RUQ US, TVUS, TTE, TEE**: ultrasound variants, echo (transthoracic/transesophageal)
- **PET/CT, SPECT, V/Q scan**
- **Findings**: incidental, indeterminate, suspicious, BIRADS (breast), LI-RADS (liver), TI-RADS (thyroid), Lung-RADS

## Critical/panic values — must be acted on

- K >6.5 or <2.5
- Na >160 or <120
- Glucose >500 or <40
- Troponin elevation with symptoms
- INR >5 on anticoagulation
- Hgb <7 with symptoms
- Plt <20
- Positive blood cultures
- New malignancy on imaging
- Acute intracranial findings (bleed, mass, stroke)
- Pulmonary embolism
- Aortic dissection / aneurysm rupture

Always check: was the abnormal value **acknowledged and acted upon** in a subsequent note?
