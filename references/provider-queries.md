# Provider Query Templates (Compliant, Non-Leading)

Provider queries clarify, augment, or correct documentation. They must follow **ACDIS/AHIMA Practice Brief: Guidelines for Achieving a Compliant Query Practice (2022)**.

## Rules

1. **Non-leading**: do not suggest a single answer that maximizes reimbursement.
2. **Multiple choice** with clinically reasonable options, OR open-ended.
3. **Clinically supported**: include the indicators from the record that prompted the query.
4. **Documented**: query and response become part of the medical record.
5. **No yes/no queries** unless confirming a documented diagnosis that needs explicit statement.
6. **Avoid** queries that introduce new information not in the record.

## Template — Multiple Choice (specificity)

> **Patient**: [identifier]
> **Encounter / DOS**: [date]
> **Clinical indicators in the record**:
> - [Lab: e.g., BNP 1850]
> - [Imaging: e.g., CXR with pulmonary edema]
> - [Treatment: e.g., IV furosemide drip]
> - [Exam: e.g., bibasilar crackles, JVD]
>
> **Query**: The above findings suggest the patient may have heart failure. If clinically applicable, please further specify the type and acuity:
>
> - [ ] Acute systolic (HFrEF) heart failure
> - [ ] Acute diastolic (HFpEF) heart failure
> - [ ] Acute on chronic systolic heart failure
> - [ ] Acute on chronic diastolic heart failure
> - [ ] Chronic systolic heart failure
> - [ ] Chronic diastolic heart failure
> - [ ] Other: __________
> - [ ] Unable to determine
> - [ ] Not clinically applicable
>
> Please document your response in the medical record.

## Template — Open-Ended (clarification)

> **Clinical indicators**: [list]
> **Query**: Based on the above, please clarify in your documentation the etiology and clinical significance of [finding].

## Template — Conflicting documentation

> The record contains conflicting documentation regarding [condition]:
> - [Source A — e.g., H&P states "pneumonia"]
> - [Source B — e.g., Discharge summary states "bronchitis"]
>
> Please clarify the final, accurate diagnosis and document accordingly.

## Template — Clinical validation (e.g., sepsis)

> **Clinical indicators**: T 39.1, HR 118, RR 24, WBC 18.5, lactate 3.2, positive blood culture, started on IV vancomycin + cefepime.
> **Query**: The record documents "bacteremia" but the above indicators may also support a more specific diagnosis. Please confirm whether the patient meets criteria for:
>
> - [ ] Sepsis without organ dysfunction
> - [ ] Sepsis with acute organ dysfunction (severe sepsis / sepsis-3)
> - [ ] Septic shock
> - [ ] Bacteremia only, without sepsis
> - [ ] Other: __________
> - [ ] Unable to determine

## Anti-patterns (these are non-compliant)

- "The patient has acute systolic heart failure, correct?" → leading
- "Please add HCC J44.1 to support risk adjustment." → coding-focused, not clinical
- "Was the AKI due to sepsis (this would increase the DRG weight)?" → mentions financial impact
- Queries without clinical indicators
- Queries that introduce diagnoses not supported by the chart
