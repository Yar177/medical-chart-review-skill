# Clinical Abbreviations Cheat Sheet

> Always confirm in context — many abbreviations are ambiguous (e.g., "MS" = multiple sclerosis / mitral stenosis / morphine sulfate / mental status). The Joint Commission "Do Not Use" list bans some abbreviations from medical records.

## General
- **Pt** patient · **Pts** patients · **F/U** follow-up · **R/O** rule out · **w/** with · **w/o** without
- **c/o** complains of · **s/p** status post · **h/o** history of · **PMH** past medical hx · **PSH** past surgical hx
- **FH** family history · **SH** social history · **ROS** review of systems · **PE** physical exam
- **HPI** history of present illness · **CC** chief complaint · **A/P** assessment & plan
- **DOS** date of service · **DOA** date of admission · **DOB** date of birth · **DOD** date of discharge (or death)
- **LOS** length of stay · **DC** / **D/C** discharge (or discontinue — context!)

## Vitals & exam
- **BP** blood pressure · **HR** heart rate · **RR** respiratory rate · **T** temp · **SpO2** pulse ox
- **BMI** body mass index · **Wt** weight · **Ht** height
- **A&Ox3/4** alert & oriented · **NAD** no acute distress · **WDWN** well-developed well-nourished
- **CTAB** clear to auscultation bilaterally · **RRR** regular rate and rhythm · **S1S2** heart sounds
- **NT/ND** non-tender, non-distended · **+BS** bowel sounds present · **NCAT** normocephalic atraumatic
- **EOMI** extraocular muscles intact · **PERRL** pupils equal round reactive to light

## Symptoms
- **SOB** shortness of breath · **DOE** dyspnea on exertion · **CP** chest pain · **N/V** nausea/vomiting · **N/V/D** + diarrhea
- **HA** headache · **LBP** low back pain · **LOC** loss of consciousness · **AMS** altered mental status
- **HEENT** head/eyes/ears/nose/throat

## Conditions
- **HTN** hypertension · **HLD** hyperlipidemia · **DM/T2DM/T1DM** diabetes · **CAD** coronary artery dz
- **CHF/HFrEF/HFpEF** heart failure · **AFib** atrial fib · **MI/STEMI/NSTEMI** myocardial infarction
- **COPD** chronic obstructive pulm dz · **OSA** obstructive sleep apnea · **PNA** pneumonia
- **CKD/ESRD** kidney disease · **UTI** urinary tract infection · **BPH** benign prostatic hyperplasia
- **GERD** gastroesophageal reflux · **IBD/UC/CD** inflammatory bowel/ulcerative colitis/Crohn's
- **CVA/TIA** stroke / transient ischemic attack · **MS** multiple sclerosis (context)
- **MDD** major depressive disorder · **GAD** generalized anxiety disorder · **PTSD** · **SUD** substance use disorder
- **OA/RA** osteo/rheumatoid arthritis · **DVT/PE** venous thromboembolism

## Meds & routes
- **PO** by mouth · **IV** intravenous · **IM** intramuscular · **SQ/SC** subcutaneous · **PR** per rectum · **SL** sublingual
- **BID/TID/QID** twice/thrice/four-times daily · **QD** daily (avoid per JCAHO; use "daily") · **QHS** at bedtime · **PRN** as needed
- **mcg** (avoid "μg") · **units** (spell out — "U" is on Do-Not-Use list)

## Joint Commission "Do Not Use" list (flag these!)
- **U/u** for unit → spell out "unit"
- **IU** for international unit → spell out
- **Q.D., QD, q.d., qd** → spell "daily"
- **Q.O.D., QOD** → spell "every other day"
- **Trailing zero** (X.0 mg) and **lack of leading zero** (.X mg) for medication doses
- **MS / MSO4 / MgSO4** → spell out morphine sulfate or magnesium sulfate

## Coding/admin
- **ICD-10-CM / ICD-10-PCS** diagnosis / procedure codes
- **CPT / HCPCS** procedure / supply codes
- **DRG / MS-DRG** diagnosis-related group
- **HCC** hierarchical condition category · **RAF** risk adjustment factor
- **CDI** clinical documentation integrity · **UR** utilization review
- **POA** present on admission · **HAC** hospital-acquired condition · **HAI** hospital-acquired infection
- **EHR/EMR** · **PHI** protected health info · **BAA** business associate agreement
